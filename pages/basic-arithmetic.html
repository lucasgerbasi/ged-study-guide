Basic Arithmetic

What You Need to Know:

Overview

Basic Arithmetic is a core component of the GED Mathematics test. It tests your understanding of numbers, operations, and basic mathematical principles. Topics include addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, fractions, decimals, percentages, and word problems.

Key Topics:

  • Addition and Subtraction
  • Multiplication and Division
  • Fractions
  • Decimals
  • Percentages
  • Word Problems

Addition and Subtraction

Addition: The process of finding the total or sum by combining two or more numbers.

Subtraction: The process of finding the difference between two numbers by removing one number from another.

Multiplication and Division

Multiplication: The process of repeated addition.

Division: The process of splitting a number into equal parts.

Fractions, Decimals, and Percentages

These are different ways to represent parts of a whole. You should be comfortable converting between them.

  • Fraction: Represents a part of a whole (e.g., 1/2).
  • Decimal: Another way to write a fraction (e.g., 0.5).
  • Percentage: A fraction of 100 (e.g., 50%).

Word Problems

These problems involve real-life situations that require applying arithmetic operations to solve. Identify the key information, translate it into a mathematical expression, and solve.

Quiz Questions:

1. What is the sum of 587 and 438?

2. Subtract 365 from 920. What is the result?

3. Multiply 25 by 12. What is the product?

4. Divide 144 by 12. What is the quotient?

5. Which of the following is equivalent to 3/4?

6. Simplify the fraction 12/36.

7. Convert 0.4 to a fraction in simplest form.

8. What is 25% of 200?

9. A store is offering a 15% discount on an item that costs $80. How much is the discount?

10. What is the total of 54, 32, and 18?

11. Divide 156 by 13. What is the quotient?

12. What is the difference between 528 and 273?

13. What is the value of 0.25 + 0.75?

14. Multiply 16 by 9. What is the product?

15. Which of the following is equal to 7/10?

16. Convert 50% to a decimal. What is the decimal equivalent?

17. A recipe calls for 3/4 cup of sugar. If you want to make half the recipe, how much sugar will you need?

18. Add 4.75 to 3.25. What is the sum?

19. Which of the following is equal to 10%?

20. Subtract 75 from 150. What is the result?
