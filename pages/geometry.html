Geometry

What You Need to Know:

I. Basic Geometry Concepts

Points, Lines, and Angles

Point: A location in space with no size or dimension.

Line: A straight path extending infinitely in both directions, with no thickness.

Line Segment: A part of a line with two endpoints.

Ray: A part of a line that starts at one point and extends infinitely in one direction.

Angle: Formed by two rays with a common endpoint (the vertex).

Acute Angle: Less than 90 degrees.

Right Angle: Exactly 90 degrees.

Obtuse Angle: More than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees.

Straight Angle: Exactly 180 degrees.

Complementary Angles: Two angles that add up to 90 degrees.

Supplementary Angles: Two angles that add up to 180 degrees.

Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

Parallel Lines: Lines that are always the same distance apart and never meet.

Perpendicular Lines: Lines that intersect at a 90-degree angle.

Polygons

A polygon is a closed figure with straight sides. The number of sides determines the type:

  • Triangle: 3 sides
  • Quadrilateral: 4 sides
  • Pentagon: 5 sides
  • Hexagon: 6 sides
  • Heptagon: 7 sides
  • Octagon: 8 sides

Regular Polygon: All sides and angles are equal.

Irregular Polygon: Sides and angles are not equal.

Circle

Radius: The distance from the center to any point on the circle.

Diameter: The distance across the circle, passing through the center (diameter = 2 × radius).

Circumference: The perimeter of the circle. Formula: C = 2πr

Area of Circle: The space inside the circle. Formula: A = πr²

II. Triangles

Types of Triangles
  • Equilateral Triangle: All sides and angles are equal (each angle = 60°).
  • Isosceles Triangle: Two sides are equal, and the angles opposite those sides are equal.
  • Scalene Triangle: All sides and angles are different.
  • Right Triangle: One angle is 90°.
  • Acute Triangle: All angles are less than 90°.
  • Obtuse Triangle: One angle is greater than 90°.
Pythagorean Theorem (for Right Triangles)

States that in a right triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides.

Formula: a² + b² = c² (where a and b are the legs of the triangle, and c is the hypotenuse).

Area and Perimeter of a Triangle

Area: A = ½ × base × height

Perimeter: Sum of the lengths of all three sides.

III. Quadrilaterals

Types of Quadrilaterals
  • Square: All sides are equal, and all angles are 90°.
  • Rectangle: Opposite sides are equal, and all angles are 90°.
  • Rhombus: All sides are equal, but the angles are not 90°.
  • Parallelogram: Opposite sides are parallel and equal in length.
  • Trapezoid: One pair of opposite sides are parallel.
Area and Perimeter of Quadrilaterals

Square: Area = A = s², Perimeter = P = 4s

Rectangle: Area = A = l × w, Perimeter = P = 2l + 2w

Parallelogram: Area = A = base × height

Trapezoid: Area = A = ½ × (b₁ + b₂) × h

IV. Circles and Their Properties

Circumference: C = 2πr

Area of Circle: A = πr²

Arc Length

The length of an arc is a fraction of the circle's circumference, proportional to the central angle. Formula: Arc Length = (θ / 360) × 2πr

Sector Area

The area of a sector is a fraction of the area of the circle, proportional to the central angle. Formula: Sector Area = (θ / 360) × πr²

V. Coordinate Geometry

Distance Formula

Formula: d = √((x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)²)

Midpoint Formula

Formula: Midpoint = ((x₁ + x₂) / 2, (y₁ + y₂) / 2)

VI. Solid Geometry

Volume and Surface Area of 3D Shapes

Cube: Volume = V = s³, Surface Area = A = 6s²

Rectangular Prism: Volume = V = l × w × h, Surface Area = A = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh

Sphere: Volume = V = (4/3)πr³, Surface Area = A = 4πr²

Cylinder: Volume = V = πr²h, Surface Area = A = 2πr² + 2πrh

Cone: Volume = V = (1/3)πr²h, Surface Area = A = πr(r + √(r² + h²))

VII. Key Formulas to Remember

  • Area of a Rectangle: A = l × w
  • Area of a Triangle: A = ½ × base × height
  • Area of a Circle: A = πr²
  • Pythagorean Theorem: a² + b² = c²
  • Circumference of a Circle: C = 2πr
  • Volume of a Cube: V = s³
  • Volume of a Rectangular Prism: V = l × w × h
  • Surface Area of a Sphere: A = 4πr²
