Solving Linear Equations

A linear equation is an equation where the variable is raised to the first power and the equation forms a straight line when graphed. The goal is to solve for the variable.

Example:

Solve for 𝑥: 2𝑥 + 5 = 15

  • Subtract 5 from both sides: 2𝑥 = 10
  • Divide both sides by 2: 𝑥 = 5

Solving Inequalities

Inequalities express a range of values instead of a single value. The symbols used are: <, >, ≤, ≥, and ≠.

< (Less Than):

  • This symbol means that the value on the left side is smaller than the value on the right side.
  • Example: 3 < 5 means 3 is less than 5.

> (Greater Than):

  • This symbol means that the value on the left side is larger than the value on the right side.
  • Example: 7 > 4 means 7 is greater than 4.

≤ (Less Than or Equal To):

  • This symbol means that the value on the left side is either smaller than or equal to the value on the right side.
  • Example: 6 ≤ 6 means 6 is less than or equal to 6.

≥ (Greater Than or Equal To):

  • This symbol means that the value on the left side is either larger than or equal to the value on the right side.
  • Example: 8 ≥ 8 means 8 is greater than or equal to 8.

≠ (Not Equal To):

  • This symbol means that the value on the left side is not equal to the value on the right side.
  • Example: 6 ≠ 9 means 6 is not equal to 9.

The process is similar to solving linear equations, but remember to flip the inequality sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative number.

Example:

Solve for 𝑥: 3𝑥 + 4 > 10

  • Subtract 4 from both sides: 3𝑥 > 6
  • Divide both sides by 3: 𝑥 > 2

Working with Polynomials

Polynomials are expressions made up of terms with variables raised to non-negative integer powers. Common operations include addition, subtraction, multiplication, and factoring.

Example:

Simplify the expression: (3𝑥 + 4) + (2𝑥 − 5)

  • Combine like terms: 3𝑥 + 2𝑥 + 4 − 5 = 5𝑥 − 1

Factoring Quadratic Equations

Quadratic equations have the form 𝑎𝑥² + 𝑏𝑥 + 𝑐 = 0. Factoring involves rewriting the equation as a product of binomials.

Example:

Factor 𝑥² + 5𝑥 + 6

  • Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add up to 5: 2 and 3.
  • So, 𝑥² + 5𝑥 + 6 = (𝑥 + 2)(𝑥 + 3)

Functions

A function is a relation where each input has one output. Functions are often written as 𝑓(𝑥), where 𝑥 is the input, and 𝑓(𝑥) is the output.

Example:

If 𝑓(𝑥) = 2𝑥 + 3, find 𝑓(4): 𝑓(4) = 2(4) + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11

Solving Systems of Equations

A system of equations consists of two or more equations with the same set of variables. The solution is the point where the equations intersect.

Example:

Solve the system of equations: 𝑥 + 𝑦 = 5, 𝑥 − 𝑦 = 1

  • Add the two equations: 2𝑥 = 6, so 𝑥 = 3
  • Substitute 𝑥 = 3 into 𝑥 + 𝑦 = 5: 3 + 𝑦 = 5, so 𝑦 = 2
  • The solution is 𝑥 = 3, 𝑦 = 2
